# Shamba — Startup Execution Plan

## The core bet
Shamba wins if this one assumption is true:
> A Kenyan smallholder farmer with a mobile phone will complete a digital loan application when they know the funds arrive as a voucher at their trusted local agro-dealer — not cash they might misuse.

Everything else (insurance, marketplace, groups) is upsell. Test this first.

## Week 1–2: First 10 farmers
DO NOT build anything new. Instead:

1. Go to the Nakuru Maize Growers SACCO (already in your seed data as a template)
2. Find the real group — any farmers' group in your county with 20+ members
3. Meet the chairperson. Show them the app on your phone.
4. Sign up 10 farmers personally, one by one. Help them fill the profile.
5. Run a mock loan application for each. Show them the voucher.

What you're testing: do they complete it? Do they understand vouchers?

## Week 3–4: First real loan
Partner with ONE agro-dealer in Nakuru/Kiambu:
- Walk in. Show them the system. Explain: farmer brings voucher code, you supply inputs, we pay you M-Pesa within 24hrs.
- Most agro-dealers will say yes immediately — it solves their credit risk problem too.
- Run ONE real loan. KES 5,000–10,000. Your own money or a friend's.
- Document everything.

## Month 2: Insurance + marketplace
Only after loan #1 succeeds:
- Add parametric insurance to that farmer's next loan (bundle it automatically)
- List their harvest on the marketplace
- Find one buyer (a small restaurant, school canteen, hotel)

## Investor pitch (Month 3+)
With 10 active farmers, 1 real loan repaid, and 1 marketplace transaction:
- You have proof of concept
- You can pitch: "We've done X in 60 days with zero marketing spend"
- Target: Mercy Corps, Safaricom Spark, Shell Foundation, GSMA AgriTech

## The unfair advantage you have right now
1. The full system is built — competitors spent $200K+ getting here
2. You understand the Kenyan farming context deeply (Nakuru, Kiambu, Uasin Gishu)
3. The USSD channel means feature phone farmers can use it — most competitors skip this
4. Multi-tenant architecture means you can white-label to cooperatives and MFIs immediately

## What NOT to do
- Don't wait for M-Pesa Daraja approval before testing (use manual M-Pesa payments)
- Don't build Uganda/Tanzania features before Kenya works
- Don't spend on ads before you have 100 farmers who love the product
- Don't hire anyone before first loan repaid

## The 3 metrics that matter in year 1
1. Loan repayment rate (must be >85% or the business doesn't work)
2. Farmer profile completion rate (drives credit score quality)
3. Days to first marketplace sale per farmer (shorter = higher LTV)
